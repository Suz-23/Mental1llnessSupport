What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a mental illness where the person is seen to become disconnected to reality. Schizophrenia is mostly common among the ages between 16 and 30, after experiencing their first psychosis.

Causes and Effects of Schizophrenia

Causes:

Though the exact cause of Schizophrenia is still unknown, some scientist suspect that Schizophrenia occurs due to their genetics, or environmental, and brain-related factors.

Symptoms:

Physical symptoms of schizophrenia are, unusual motor behavior, though these behaviors differs by individuals. Disorganized speech and thinking, where the person may ask questions that does not connect to the topic, or use unrelated words that could not be understood.

Phychological symptoms of schizophrenia are, Delusions, for example a person could have a belief that they are going to get harrassed or attcacked. Hallucinations, which connects to the delusion symptom, where hallucination makes the delution more realistic to the person.
