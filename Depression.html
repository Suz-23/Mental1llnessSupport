What is Depression

Depression is a mental illness where a person have long term feeling of sadness or loss of interest. Though many people think that depression is something to not take seriously, it could still lead to major conflicts like suicide or self-harm. It is also indicated that about 7% of adults in united states experiences depression every year.

Types of Depressions

Clinical Depression:

Clinical Depression, also known as major depressive disorder, is a more severe type of dressppression. Though it still have symptoms such as loss of interest, or less apitite.

Persistent Depressive disorder (PDD):

Persistent Depressive Disorder also known as PDD is a type of depression where, the feeling of sadness, and doesn't dissapear forever.

Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder (DMDD):

Disrupive mood dysregulation disorder also known as DMDD is a type of depression that. Typically in younger ages around 10 years old, though many healthcare providers does not diagnose people under 6 or over 18 years old with this type of depression. Leading to our understanding that DMDD does not diagnose infants or adults. Unlike the other types of depression like Clinical or Presistent depressions, DMDDs' symptoms are not sadness, instead it has the symptoms of short temper and temper burst or angery moods.

Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD):

Premenstrual dysphoric disorder, also known as PMDD is a type of depression where a person may feel strong anger, suicidal thoughts, insomnia, or overwhelming. Or have strong mood swings. This is commonly seen in females before their periods due to hormone changes in body, and or neurotransmitter imbalances.

Causes and Effects of Depression

Causes:

Just like many other mental illnesses, causes are still unknown, since different people have different triggers. Though some factors that contribute to its developement is imbalace of neoutransmitters, like serotonin and dopamine. Genetices, if a person has a relative with depression, it is indicated that the person will have three times as likely to develop depression. Or Stressful events like divorce, isolation, or lack of support from others. And some medical conditions such as, diabete or medications could cause depression as a side effect.

Symptoms:

Physical symtoms are mass weight loss or weight gain, due to eating too much or too little, stomachache, headaches, feeling of physical dredness, trouble sleeping.

Phychological symptoms are, loss of interest towards hobbies, and things that brought joy before. Feeling of sadness, hoplessness. Or without any help, it could lead to suicidal thoughts self-harm, or harm towards others.

Inequallity That People with Depression Face

Inequallity that people with depression face is acknowledgement about the difference between, sadness and depression. Sadness is a normal and temporary emotion of disadvantage, loss, despair, grief, helplessness, disappointment and sorrow. On the other hand, depression is a longer term of that feeling. Sadness is not a mood disorder though Depression is. Depression could lead to how people think, act and feel, though normal sadness doesn't. Though sadness could become depression, it is not the same as depression. Because of this, many people, like other mental illnesses treat depression as a shorter term, non-harmful thing. Many categorize depression as "something that could easily be solved" eventhough that fact is a false. Without any treatment depression could lead to suicide, substance use, and or unstable physical health.
