What is Depression

DDepression is a mental illness in which a person has a long term feeling of sadness or loss of interest. Though many people think that depression is something to not take seriously, it could still lead to major conflicts like suicide or self-harm. A federal agency for researching mental illnesses, National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) states that about 18% of 18 - 25 year olds are diagnosed with depression in the United States every year.

Types of Depressions

Clinical Depression:

Clinical Depression, also known as major depressive disorder, is a more severe type of dressppression. Though it still have symptoms such as loss of interest, or less apitite.

Persistent Depressive disorder (PDD):

Persistent Depressive Disorder also known as PDD is a type of depression where, the feeling of sadness, and doesn't dissapear forever.

Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder (DMDD):

Disruptive mood dysregulation disorder also known as DMDD is a type of depression that is typically diagnosed at younger ages around 10 years old. Though many healthcare providers do not diagnose people under 6 or over 18 years old with this type of depression. Leading to our understanding that DMDD does not diagnose infants or adults. Unlike the other types of depression like Clinical or Persistent depressions, DMDDs' symptoms are not sadness or feeling dreaded. Instead it has the symptoms of short temper and temper burst or angry moods.

Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD):

Premenstrual dysphoric disorder, also known as PMDD is a similar type of depression where a person may feel strong anger, suicidal thoughts, insomnia, or overwhelming and or have strong mood swings. Though PMDD occurs at all ages, however, according to Cleveland Clinic, PMDD is commonly seen in females before their periods due to hormone changes in body, and or neurotransmitter imbalances.

Causes and Effects of Depression

Causes:

Just like many other mental illnesses, causes are still unknown. This is because different people have different triggers. According to a clinical psychologist, Paul Everett, an imbalance of neurotransmitters, like serotonin and dopamine or genetics, could cause development of depression. He stated that if a person had or has a relative with depression, it is indicated that the person will have three times as likely to develop depression. However, stressful events like divorce, isolation, or lack of support from others could and some medical conditions such as diabetes or medications could cause depression as a side effect, also lead to the development of depression.

Symptoms:

Behavioral symptoms of depression include: significant weight loss or weight gain, due to eating too much or too little, stomachache, headaches, feeling of physical tiredness, trouble sleeping.

Psychological symptoms of depression include: loss of interest towards hobbies, and things that brought joy before. Feeling of sadness, hopelessness. Or without any help, it could lead to suicidal thoughts self-harm, or harm towards others.

Inequality That People with Depression Face

Inequality that people with depression face is getting no acknowledgement about the difference between sadness and depression. Many people believe sadness and depression are the same, but this belief is harmful and incorrect. Sadness is a normal and temporary emotion of disadvantage, loss, despair, grief, helplessness, disappointment and sorrow. On the other hand, depression is a long term feeling of loss, despair, grief, helplessness, disappointment and sorrow . Sadness is not a mood disorder though depression is. Depression could lead to how people think, act and feel in a harmful way like suicidal thoughts, though sadness doesn't. However sadness could become depression, sadness is in fact one of the psychological symptoms of depression. Though it is not the same as depression. Because of this, many people treat depression as a non-harmful thing and can be "easily cured”. This leads to statements like “Just cheer up” or “Everyone gets sad,” which can make people with depression feel even more alone and feel unsupported. Without any treatment depression could lead to suicide, substance use, and or unstable physical health.

Solutions to Inequality Towards People with Depression

To reduce this inequality, schools and communities should teach the real differences between sadness and depression, but also an in depth description about the effects depression could have on us as a community. In Social Issues, we are already educated about what and how sadness and depression are different. However the bigger effects of depression are still not discussed. Just like it was written at symptoms colum of this website, it could lead to many negative thoughts or actions like trouble sleeping ot suicidal thoughts, with this people with depression without any help may have harder time at school, or at home, in their community, and more. But educating kids about the effects of depression, it could lead to people giving more support and help towards people with depression.
