What is PTSD?

PTSD, also known as Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder is a mental illness where a person who have witnessed or been apart of traggic events, or any type of trauma, experiencing those tramuas as emotionally, physicially, or life threating event. Though it is commonly sterotyped that solders or people who have been apart of war having PTSD. Though this is a false idea, anyone that have gone through tramatic events are likely to have PTSD.

Cause and Effects of PTSD

Causes:

Unlike the other mental illnesses, PTSD is caused by tramuatic events. Some examples of this are, wars, sexual and physical assaults, natural dissasters, terror attack, abuse, or accidents like car crash. Though not everyone who have went through a tramuatic event goes through PTSD. Though the reasons why tramuatic events cause PTSD is since, your body goes in a fight or fright position, trying to avoid any kind of danger or harm.

Symptoms:

Behavioral include: feeling on guard or on edge, difficulty consentrating, aggersive outburst, becoming more easily startled, and acting on reckless or risky behaviors.

Phychological symptoms include: Feeling of distress or anxiety when facing anything that triggers their trauma, or having negative thougts about one another, Exaggerated feelings of blame towards others, loss of interest in previous hobbies or activities, and Feelings of social isolation.

Inequallity That People With PTSD Face

Inequality that people with PTSD face is romaticizing that PTSD occurs only to soliders, and not others. In many cases, people romantasize PTSD only occuring to soliders, according to the U.S Department of veteran Affairs it is stated that about 23% percent of veterans exprience PTSD at some point. Due to an understanding that “only soliders have PTSD” people with PTSD that are not veterans are treated without any support or even doubted and asked questions such as “you weren’t in a war, do you actually have PTSD?” which could lead to people with PTSD to feel invalidated and isolated.

Solutions to Inequallity Towards People with PTSD

The solution to these inequalities is to educate people about PTSD in-depth. By doing this, the stereotype that PTSD not only occurs to veterans will be understood. Educating others about PTSD could help the person with PTSD, instead of ealier diagnosis. Some ways of in-depth education could be a lesson dedicated to PTSD, and its causes and effects on a person. Or a social media campegin where it raises money for people with PTSD but also explaining what PTSD and how PTSD is formed.
