What is Paranoia?

Paranoia is a type of mental illness thats characterized by people having irrational distrust or suspicion of others, and having a fear of being tricked or being taken advantage of.

Main Types of Paranoia

Paranoid Personality Disorder

Paranoid personality disorder is a type of paranoia, where a person have a strong suspicion, or distrust towards others, limiting them from a stable social life. Some symptoms of PPD include, Concerns that other people have hidden motives or bring harm to themselves, Social Issolation, and Detachments.

Delusional (paranoid) Disorder

Delusional (paranoid) disorder is a type of paranoia, where a person believes a one dominant delusion or a false idea without any other signs of mental illnesses. Though the symptoms differs from person to person due to their delusions changing their day to day behaviors. For example if someone has a false belief that someone is stalking them the indivial may act more hidden or aware of their surroundings.

Causes and Effects of Paranoia

Causes:

Some of causes of paranoia are, uses of drugs such as cannabus, amphetamine, cocaine or ecstasy and withdraw to these drugs, past traumas like child abuse, domestic violence, and any type of discrimination such as racism, homophobia or sexism. Or feeling of lonliness, or exclusion could also lead to the cause of Paranoia.

Symptoms:

Behavioral symptoms of paranoia includes, hostil actions and doubts towards others.

Phychological symptoms of paranoia includes, difficulty trusting others, amusptions about others talking behind their backs. Or difficulty copeing with critism from others.

Inequallity That People with Paranoia Face

Inequality that people with paranoia face is being assumed that they are being “crazy”. One of the symptoms of paranoia which is doubts towards others or difficulty trusting others, leads to many people stereotyping people with paranoia as “crazy” or “overthinking” due to this stereotype, it could lead to the person to self-doubt or be avoided by others leading to social isolation.

Solutions to Inequallity Towards People with Paranoia

Solutions to these inequalities is to understand that people with paranoia are not being crazy or over reacting and including those people instead of isolating them or avoiding them. From this solution, the people with paranoia will feel more included, comfortable, and trusted.
