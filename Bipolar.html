What is Bipolar?

Bipolar disorder is a mood disorder, which causes intense mood swings, which could last for hours, days, or months, it can also have an effect on one another's behaviors. The person experiences intence and high feeling called manias, and lower, less intence feelin called depression.

Causes and Effects of Bipolar

Causes:

Just like other mental illnesses, the specific causes of bipolar are unknown, though some things that trigger bipolar could be environmental changes, psychotic disorders, or substance misuse. It is also scientifically proven that people with a family history of experiencing mood disorders have higher chances to have bipolar disorder.

Symptoms:

Physical symtomps incude, increased activity, for example, working on several projects at once, and having no time to rest. It could also lead to increase in risky and impulsive behavior such as, spending sprees, and reckless or drunk driving. Getting less sleep is also the physical symtomps of bipolar.

Psychological symtomps include, racing thoughts, and increased speech. Distractibility, which is a state where you are easily distracted. Some studies suggest that when diagnosed with bipolar, to have more active feeling with short temper or irritable.

Inequallity That People with Bipolar Face

Some Inequalities that people with bipolar faces is, being misunderstood from being “moody” or “emotional” instead of being diagnosed with bipolar. Since Bipolar is a mood disorder, and has symptoms such as short temper or impulsive behaviors, some categorize people with bipolar as being “moody” or “emotional”. This leads to many people feeling invalidated or stereotyped, and could lead to the person not asking for help.

Solutions to these Inequalities

Solutions to these Inequality for people with bipolar is, educating people about bipolar disorder, and having an understanding that bipolar is not always being “moody” or “emotional” and needs to be taken seriously.
